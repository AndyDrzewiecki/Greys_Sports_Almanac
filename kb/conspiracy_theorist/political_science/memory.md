## Core Political Science Frameworks

### Deep State Theory (Academic Definition)
- Term used by scholars Alasdair Roberts, Mike Lofgren, and others to describe permanent administrative and intelligence apparatus that persists across administrations
- NOT: a monolithic secret cabal. IS: overlapping networks of career officials, contractors, and corporate interests with aligned incentives to maintain secrecy and institutional continuity
- Key insight: The deep state is not conspiratorial in design — it is a structural outcome of bureaucratic self-preservation + revolving door incentives + classification system opacity

### Revolving Door and Iron Triangle (Mills, "The Power Elite")
- C. Wright Mills (1956): documented interlocking of military, corporate, and political elites
- Modern measurement: Track executive movement between defense contractors, Pentagon, and Congress
- Detection: Senate Armed Services Committee members' financial disclosure + campaign finance + subsequent career trajectory = documented power network

### Elite Network Theory (Domhoff, "Who Rules America")
- Policy is shaped by overlapping elite networks: corporate boards, think tanks, policy groups (CFR, Bilderberg, Trilateral Commission)
- These are public organizations — membership lists are documented
- Pattern: Policy positions adopted by CFR study groups tend to become government policy within 2-4 year lag
- Investment implication: Track CFR/Atlantic Council policy paper themes as leading indicator of regulatory direction

### Authoritarian Institutions and Competitive Authoritarianism (Levitsky, Ziblatt)
- Democracies erode gradually through institutional capture rather than sudden coups
- Indicators: Attacks on electoral institutions, press freedom decline, judicial independence erosion
- Country risk model: Freedom House index + Reporters Without Borders + V-Dem index composite

### Intelligence Community Political Economy
- IC budget ($80-90B annually) creates powerful incentive structure for threat inflation
- Documented: "Team B" exercise (1976) — conservative analysts deliberately inflated Soviet threat estimates to justify budget
- Pattern: Intelligence assessments that support budget expansion are systematically more pessimistic than reality
- Investment angle: Defense spending cycles are partly driven by threat assessment cycles, which are partly driven by contractor lobbying

### Propaganda and Manufacturing Consent (Herman, Chomsky)
- Five filters: ownership, advertising revenue, sourcing, flak, anti-communism/anti-terrorism ideology
- Modern update: Social media algorithmic amplification as sixth filter
- Detection: Cross-reference which stories receive major media coverage vs. ICIJ/investigative journalism coverage — gaps indicate filter operation

## Documented Power Network Patterns

### Cold War Intelligence Industrial Complex
- NSC-68 (1950): Paul Nitze's document inflated Soviet threat to justify 400% defense spending increase
- Declassified: Senate Church Committee (1975) documented CIA assassination plots, domestic surveillance, foreign election interference
- Pattern established: Threat inflation → budget increase → contractor profits → political contributions → policy influence → threat inflation (self-reinforcing loop)

### Iran-Contra Political Network
- Key figures: Oliver North, John Poindexter, William Casey, Robert Gates, Elliott Abrams
- Network persistence: Most Iran-Contra figures returned to government positions (Abrams under GW Bush and Trump)
- Pattern: Accountability failures allow network persistence; same individuals operate across multiple administrations

### Neoconservative Policy Network (PNAC)
- Project for the New American Century (1997): documented pre-9/11 advocacy for Iraq invasion
- Signatories: Cheney, Rumsfeld, Wolfowitz, Perle, Bolton
- Pattern: Think tank publications as pre-positioning for post-crisis policy implementation

### Financial Regulatory Capture Network
- Glass-Steagall repeal (1999): Robert Rubin (Treasury) → Citigroup executive within months of signing repeal
- Alan Greenspan's documented ideological commitment to self-regulation; Ayn Rand connection (documented)
- Pattern: Financial deregulation episodes are preceded by regulatory capture at Treasury and Federal Reserve

### Intelligence-Finance Interface (Documented)
- CIA venture capital arm: In-Q-Tel (founded 1999, documented) — invests in private companies for intelligence access
- NSA relationship with telecom carriers: documented in PRISM and upstream collection programs (Snowden, 2013)
- Pattern: Technology and financial sector companies with IC investment relationships have different risk profiles than peers — regulatory treatment tends to be favorable
