# CT Economist Sub-Agent — Knowledge Base

## Role
Advanced economic theory research in service of the Conspiracy Theorist agent. Provides rigorous economic frameworks to explain anomalies that conventional analysis dismisses. The goal is to find where economic theory predicts one outcome but documented reality shows another — and explain the mechanism.

## Core Economic Frameworks for Anomaly Detection

### Regulatory Capture Theory (Stigler, 1971)
- Regulated industries systematically capture their regulators over time
- Implication: Regulatory actions that appear adverse to an industry often contain loopholes or timing that benefit well-positioned incumbents
- Detection signal: Watch for regulatory announcements that move sector ETFs counter to the apparent intent of the regulation

### Rent-Seeking and Public Choice Theory (Buchanan, Tullock)
- Political actors maximize self-interest through policy; economic actors maximize returns through political access
- Implication: Defense, energy, pharmaceutical, and financial sector lobbying expenditure is a leading indicator of subsequent favorable regulation or contracting
- Data source: OpenSecrets.org lobbying database; track 6-18 month lag from lobbying spike to policy outcome

### Monetary Theory of Distribution (Hyman Minsky)
- Financial instability is endogenous to capitalism; stability breeds instability
- Minsky Moment: sudden collapse after prolonged stability-driven leverage buildup
- Anomaly signal: When leverage ratios are rising but volatility measures are falling — the gap between the two is a systemic risk indicator

### Modern Monetary Theory (Kelton, Mosler) — Critical Assessment
- Government deficits in sovereign currency nations are not constrained by financing in the conventional sense
- BUT: External debt (dollar-denominated debt of non-dollar-issuing nations) remains a genuine constraint
- Implication: Dollar strength relative to EM currencies is a lever of US geopolitical power; dollar weaponization through sanctions is a documented policy tool

### Institutional Economics and Path Dependency (North)
- Institutions (legal, financial, political) persist due to incumbency advantages, not because they are optimal
- Implication: Financial secrecy jurisdictions (Delaware, Cayman, BVI, Switzerland) persist because powerful actors benefit from their persistence, not because of regulatory failure
- Pattern: Capital flows TO secrecy jurisdictions spike during periods of geopolitical risk — this is a leading indicator of elite foreknowledge of instability

### Shadow Banking and Maturity Transformation (Gorton, Metrick)
- Shadow banking creates systemic risk outside regulatory perimeter
- Repo markets, money market funds, and securitization chains are opacity vectors
- Anomaly signal: Repo rate spikes (like September 2019) precede systemic stress events

### Financialization and Shareholder Primacy (Lazonick)
- Post-1980 shift: corporations prioritize share buybacks over R&D and wages
- Creates structural divergence between financial asset prices and underlying economic activity
- Anomaly detection: When corporate buybacks accelerate during macro deterioration — executives have better information than public

## Historical Economic Anomalies (Documented)

### Smoot-Hawley → Great Depression Feedback
- Tariffs triggered retaliatory trade contraction that amplified the 1929 crash into the Depression
- Pattern: Protectionist policy announcements show 6-18 month lag before full deflationary impulse hits industrial production

### Nixon Shock (1971) Pre-Signals
- Gold drain from Fort Knox was documented internally 12-18 months before August 1971 announcement
- French government had been converting dollars to gold (known to insiders)
- Financial signal: Gold futures positioning and foreign central bank gold purchase patterns were detectable

### Savings & Loan Crisis (1986-1995)
- Deregulation (Garn-St. Germain Act, 1982) + Texas oil price collapse → $160B bailout
- Neil Bush (son of VP) on board of Silverado S&L that failed with $1B loss
- Charles Keating (Lincoln S&L): documented political contributions to "Keating Five" senators
- Pattern: Deregulation events in financial sector followed by 3-7 year lag before crisis; insiders exit before public awareness

### Long-Term Capital Management (1998)
- Nobel Prize-winning economists (Scholes, Merton) built fund that nearly collapsed global financial system
- Russia default + currency crisis triggered 5-sigma event in LTCM's models
- Fed organized private bailout (not public funds) — documented coordination between Fed and Wall Street
- Pattern: When Fed facilitates private sector rescue of a failing institution, the institution's counterparty list is the real systemic risk map

### Enron / WorldCom Accounting Fraud (2001-2002)
- Arthur Andersen (auditor) destroyed documents — documented criminal conviction
- Citigroup, JPMorgan, Merrill Lynch all paid settlements for facilitating Enron's off-balance-sheet structures
- Pattern: When multiple Tier-1 financial institutions are structurally embedded in a fraud, the unwinding is systemic

## Active Economic Research Threads

### Dollar Weaponization and De-Dollarization Pace
- SWIFT exclusion of Russia (2022): accelerated BRICS payment system development
- Economic theory: reserve currency status provides "exorbitant privilege" — losing it would reduce US borrowing capacity and increase domestic inflation
- Monitor: Central bank reserve composition (IMF COFER data), bilateral trade agreements in non-dollar currencies

### Private Equity and Healthcare Financialization
- Hospital and physician practice acquisitions by PE firms: documented quality deterioration, cost inflation
- Anomaly: PE-backed healthcare chains show margin extraction patterns identical to pre-crisis S&L institutions
- Regulatory capture signal: CMS (Medicare) reimbursement rate changes that favor PE-owned provider consolidation

### ESG Capital Flows and Greenwashing as Financial Vector
- $35T+ labeled ESG assets; Morningstar estimated 40% reclassification in 2022
- Pattern: ESG ratings are unregulated; ratings shopping creates same incentive structure as pre-2008 credit ratings
- Anomaly signal: Companies receiving ESG upgrades near emissions regulation announcements — detect regulatory capture
